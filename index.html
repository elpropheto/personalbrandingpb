Who Am I?

Fiction Writer

My name is Patryk Betliński, and I am a 23-year-old with a decade-long passion for writing, despite taking some breaks along the way. I take pride in my writing abilities, but my skills extend beyond the realm of words. Professionally, I work in Human Resources, where I excel in communication and have a solid understanding of labor law. This role has honed my interpersonal skills and deepened my knowledge of organizational dynamics.


Beyond my professional life, I have a diverse array of interests that keep me intellectually engaged. I am an avid programmer, constantly exploring new languages and technologies. My love for tabletop RPGs fuels my creativity and strategic thinking. Reading is a fundamental part of my life, with my literary interests spanning across astronomy, history, technology, physics, mathematics, and the stock market. Each of these fields fascinates me and contributes to my comprehensive understanding of the world.


I hold a deep appreciation for knowledge, intelligence, and continuous personal growth. I believe in the power of learning and strive to expand my horizons every day. My strengths lie in my versatility, adaptability, and eagerness to embrace new challenges. I see numerous opportunities ahead, and always strive to take advantage of them.


Looking forward, my aspirations include advancing my expertise in both HR and technology, potentially exploring the intersection of these fields to innovate and improve workplace environments. I'm also constantly working on becoming a published writer. I am driven by a desire to make meaningful contributions to whatever I undertake, whether it be in writing, my professional career, or my personal interests.


In summary, I am a multifaceted individual with a passion for learning and growth, equipped with a diverse skill set and a forward-thinking mindset. My journey is defined by a commitment to excellence and an unwavering curiosity about the world around me.

My Story

Fiction Writer

I started writing at 13 years old. I remember I was devasted that Harry Potter is "only" 7 books. I didn't want to end it there, so I started writing my own sequel - in a math notebook, during a lesson. It was obviously ridiculously bad and very short, as I couldn't force myself to write more than 10-20 pages.

During middle school I didn't write a lot, but I had tons of new ideas. Evaporated oceans as a huge jail for inmates all around the world, cruel fantasy world with guns, a love story exclusively over the phone - you name it. Everything around me was able to inspire me, but I just couldn't force myself to open Microsoft Word and start writing. Everything changed with my new, still favourite to this day, idea - a cyberpunk-ish world where video games got banned, and so the fans must play in underground organisations.

Today this book is called "Koniec Gry". I started writing it in 2018 but hated it every time I managed to start. Then COVID-19 happened and I had to stay at home, so I had many more opportunities to just start over and over... until I found one version I liked. I finished it in 2020 and never looked back.

Right now "Koniec Gry" grew to a trilogy and MANY things about it changed, but I still love it the same as I did 6 years ago. I can't wait to share it with the world.

How am I going to build my network?

Building my network as a fiction writer involves a strategic approach to connecting with readers, fellow writers, industry professionals, and influencers..

To begin with, I will focus on estabilishing online presence - create a Facebook profile and maintain my blog and/or website to post content for my potential readers. Later down the line I'm planning to actively participate in literary events and conferences, both in-person and online, to meet like-minded individuals and expand my professional circle.

I will also prioritize providing value to my network by sharing valuable content, offering mentorship, and supporting the work of others. Through consistent communication, following up on new connections, and celebrating the successes of my peers, I aim to maintain and grow these relationships, creating a supportive and vibrant network that enhances my journey as a fiction writer.

If I get lucky, I might even connect with a professional, published writer. That way the journey to my goals would be simplified, as writers are a trusted source for publishers. However, this is not my main goal. I want to get to the finish line on my own, not on someone else's back.
